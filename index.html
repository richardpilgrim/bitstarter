How often have you found yourself standing at the bus stop and looking anxiously at the stop times wondering where your bus is as it was meant to arrive 5, 10 maybe even 15 minutes ago?

Well with the power of mobile devices and crowd sourcing you can now get an accurate idea of what time the bus will arrive and how on time the bus has been in the past.

The app for smartphones uses the device's location sensors to capture a timestamp as it arrives or passes each bus stop. This is then uploaded to a server and used to estimate the time for the bus to arrive at each stop.

The process is as follows, your mobile device will detect when you are waiting or arrived at a bus stop and will alert you once you have gotten onto the bus to ask if you have boarded the bus and to confirm your bus number.

While you are riding the bus the app will check in and record a timestamp at each bus stop so others know how far away the bus is and users in the future can check previous times to know what time a bus is likely to arrive.

This app utilises location abilities available to modern smartphones as well as Google Maps Transit Data as a base data set.

All data is sent to the server anonomously so the user submitting cannot be tracked based on their phone's ID/phone number or any other personally identifiable information.

First of all let me know! I could always use more help with programming, designers and testers! I am currently a lone programmer who has done a bit of UI work so any help I can get the better this app can become.
If I was to put a monetary figure on how much this would cost then I reckon about $20000 should be the necessary capital to host the infrastructure as well as design, build and maintain the app.
Will this app be open source?
I would love to make this open source however I want to make sure all the data is collected at a central point so the maximum amount of data can be utilised. So probably open source with an API to the central repository :)
